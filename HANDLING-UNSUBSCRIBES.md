## üîç WHY PEOPLE UNSUBSCRIBE (And How to Reduce)

### **Reason 1: Wrong Target (Most Common)**

**They're not the decision maker:**
- You emailed receptionist, not owner
- They don't handle bookings/leads
- They forward to boss, but unsubscribe themselves

**Fix:**
- ‚úÖ Better targeting (owners, managers only)
- ‚úÖ Use LinkedIn to find decision makers
- ‚úÖ Verify emails are owner emails (not generic info@)

---

### **Reason 2: Too Salesy / Generic**

**Email feels like spam:**
- Generic template (no personalization)
- All about you, not them
- No clear value for their specific situation

**Fix:**
- ‚úÖ Personalize first line (mention their salon/city)
- ‚úÖ Focus on their problem, not your solution
- ‚úÖ Use specific numbers/references

---

### **Reason 3: Not Relevant**

**They don't need your service:**
- They already have lead follow-up handled
- They're not spending on ads (no leads to follow up)
- Different business model

**Fix:**
- ‚úÖ Pre-qualify better (find salons actively running ads)
- ‚úÖ Check their website (do they have booking forms?)
- ‚úÖ LinkedIn research (see if they're growing/expanding)

---

### **Reason 4: Timing**

**Wrong time to reach out:**
- Just launched (too early)
- Seasonal business (slow season)
- Recently got burned by another vendor

**Fix:**
- ‚úÖ Can't always control this
- ‚úÖ But you can note timing preferences for future

---

## üí° WAYS TO REDUCE UNSUBSCRIBES

### **1. Better Targeting**

**Before sending, verify:**
- ‚úÖ They're salon owners/managers (decision makers)
- ‚úÖ They run Google Ads or have booking forms (need your service)
- ‚úÖ Email is owner email (not generic info@salon.com)

### **2. Softer First Email**

**Option B: Soft Value (Less Aggressive)**


**Why:** Acknowledges them first, softer approach.
